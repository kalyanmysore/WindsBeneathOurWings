# Sustaining the winds of growth. 
*The Art of Starting and finishing learning/growth projects.*
## How many of us finish?
What percentage of purchased/downloaded online courses have you completed? Non-fiction paper books? Digital books? Bookmarks made while in scroll mode, for later "study mode" time?

Studies say 10-15%. (For some of us, it's worse, partly due to Udemy deals!).

Lack of time, lack of motivation, and lack of interest?

I think it is deeper than that. Writing this article is an attempt to evolve my perspective on the issue and solutions in this area.

Sources: 
* Speaking with a few others 
* Researching the topic online
* My own experience

## Days in the lives of many among us 

### A day that lights a fire under our feet
This is a rare day. Something inspires us to start a journey. Perhaps we see a new article or press release that expresses a vision that we perceive as credible.

We believe it is worth investing time and money in. Sometimes, there is a product being sold right there in the article (along with a 90% discount!). Sometimes, we have sought out the product on a different website, as step 1 of working on the vision.

We buy/borrow/subscribe without using much of our "brain" (telling ourselves that buying it is a no-brainer) ! Apple pay is so frictionless. All it takes is a jitter in our thumb to press the button twice and a zombie stare at our phone to make faceID work. And the product is sold. (Wait. Were we the product here ?)

We may install an app and login as well. We might even do a welcome session and lesson one.

Internally, this feels like we've accomplished enough for the day. After all, every 1000 mile journey starts with the first step, our mind says ! 

### A day of reckoning
This is also a rare day. Something reminds us that we're behind on one of the growth areas we thought we'd focus on. 

It maybe an email reminding us of a discounted subscription period ending or a reminder email from the library or a reminder that the next class in a series is tomorrow !

We realize that time has passed and we haven't put in the effort we thought we'd put in, when we signed up for this.

We have to reckon with the fact that we've broken a promise we made to ourselves (and perhaps spent money.). 

Quickly, the mind says it isn't worth spending time in sorrow. The mind brings up a few sayings/proverbs quickly and diverts attention away from even considering a path towards sorrow, but instead into damage control measures.

We ask for a time extension if that's possible. The mind treats this as an action/achievement. 

If no extension is possible, we resolve to just do better next time.  

If there is an actual upcoming event (a class, a presentation, a test, etc) and the deadline is not movable, do the best we can in a short time frame to prepare for the event. Perhaps we've been able to do well in many activities with just the last minute effort. Usually (not always), only we know that its not our best work, especially if the group doesn't know us well. 

The mind acts quickly during the day of reckoning, to ensure that the actual reckoning never happens ! Emotional expression which is perhaps needed for reckoning, is blocked by action.

### A usual day
For every vision, between the rare days that light our spirit and the rare days that splash cold water on our lazy face, there are the many usual days.
#### Non discretionary time
Every such day has some time that's not at our discretion. 

- Work that we've committed to at our professional place of work, kids pickup, pet care, cooking if we've committed to cook today, house/yard work or any other work that we have committed to others on.
- There is some time that members of a family spend together talking, eating, playing, etc which is theoretically discretionary but for this article, we will consider it not so.
- If we've developed habits of exercise, meditation, hobbies (in areas unrelated to growth focus areas), these are great, but not considered discretionary time for this article.
- If we have a daily commute, we might have scammed ourselves into believing that no growth content is viable during commuting.
- There maybe a social event or ticketed event that our spouse and we have committed to attending.

#### Discretionary time
And then, there are the few hours left in the day, **that are actually at our discretion**. What happens to this time ?
Mostly this:
As part of being human, we have an innate need to connect to the world. 
- Some of this desire came from school where we were praised for good "general knowledge". 
- Some from socially being rewarded for being interesting, by being able to participate in varied topics of conversation.
- There has been actual life transforming outcomes from having learned skills that are way more than what was taught in college.
- Some hobbies have started this way and have given us fulfilment

There are so many businesses that are trying to exploit this innate human need. Solo podcasters to billion dollar media companies, with their own niche topics designed to catch our attention with their storytelling.

We scroll youtube and quickly find a video that catches our attention. And then the next... Podcasters and the youtube app have got very good at eye-catching images, great storytelling and frankly, very good research and content too. 
But they spend 15 minutes of our time telling us something that we could read in 1 minute.

We may read things on twitter. If we read just the feed under our "following" tab, we might feel a FOMO and decide to just spend a few minutes on the "for you" tab.

We may read comments and the teacher in us may decide to spend time educating people (but they maybe bots !)

By the time we've interacted with the world this way, our mind may feel like it accomplished something. If the topics were heavy, it may even convince us to watch some plain entertainment drama for a while.

Hours have passed. Our mind has traveled around the world. But we've stood still on growth focus areas. And in the age of planned obsolescence, standing still is walking backwards.

We then start on one of our areas of growth. Perhaps we spend a few minutes on it. After 5 minutes, it may actually catch and hold our attention and we would have stayed for a couple of hours, if we could.

However, it is already time for one of the things on our non-discretionary list. We have to stop the growth related activity and go do the "gotta do" thing.  And getting back to the growth activity only happens after we've paid another round of donation of our attention to the billion dollar attention seeking industry.

But since we did spend a few minutes on growth activity, we feel we did all we could for the day.

## What we should learn from this
It appears that we're susceptible to time hijack when listening to podcasts or entertainment online. It can happen when reading things too.

It appears that we're good at paying attention to things but the world is hijacking this attention. We're literally "paying" attention, with our time, which is our life (potential).

We have to be careful on what we pay attention to and very carefully timebox anything that is not growth related, if we have committed to growing.


## What we should do (new behaviors)

Some of the older ones among us remember the days when there was no internet / no feed content on the internet. We'd have seek information in the form of book rental or magazine subscriptions. In both cases, we'd physically pick up something and it would finish within the time we'd estimate it would.

And we was clear on what we wanted to do and we did it (mostly).

We built stuff.

Now: 
- We have to refrain from video format news. Perhaps we should use the "AI summary" feature on youtube that can give us a text summary of a 15 minute video that we can read in 15 seconds ! And only watch the segments that we need more info on, using timestamps in the AI summary.
- We have to schedule time and do things at that time or at the earliest after.
- We have to give up on "general knowledge" and "being able to participate in conversations on various topics". We have to choose a few areas and stick to those.
- If we cant give up on "general knowledge", we have to time box it (see AI summary technique above). The timebox should be small enough to not feel like an achievement to have read the stuff.

## Previous attempts at fixing ourselves have failed
We have known that we should pay attention in areas that we would like to grow in during our discretionary time. 
That is common sense.

However, we've not really analyzed what we do. We've fallen victim to the attention grabbing industry, without even realizing that we're using up our discretionary time !

When we've attempted to do better, we've fallen off the wagon after a few days.

## How about an accountability group
Having an accountability group can help in crucial ways:
- We will be forced to reckon frequently. 
- If we got hijacked, we'll have to admit it at the very next accountability sync, which should be perhaps within a day or two?
- Knowing that we'll be held accountable will make us aware in real time, and we'll rarely waste our attention on things that dont matter.

## An ideal growth focus accountability group
- Provides humans that one is accountable to.
- Is small enough that one does not have to donate too much time listening to accounts of others. Three may be enough.
- Time boxes each participant
- Is limited to progress reports
- Is supposed to be non judgemental and encouraging
- Provides templates for the participants to have notes they bring to the meetings for their own use
- Provides frequent reckoning. Perhaps once a day for novice groups, perhaps evolving to once a week for well established groups?
- A short duration voice call is the default.
    - Can provide text based channels (whatsapp/discord group) if one is occasionally not able to attend. Occasionally only.
### Expected actions from each accountability group participant
- Attends every meeting. Atleast 80%.
- Makes notes atleast 10 min before the meeting time.
- Calls in early so that the meeting can start on time. Apologizes if late and will get some time chopped off.
- Comes ready with notes and gives an account of accomplishment/failure and next actions.
- Limits talk time to 90 sec. This perhaps allows only a few items they can state.
- When others talk, make a short, encouraging comment.
- Any roadmaps, technique sharing, knowledge sharing, discussions, etc are not part of the accountability meeting time, even if it is later done with the same group.
