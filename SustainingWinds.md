# Sustaining the winds of growth. 
*The Art of Starting and finishing learning/growth projects.*
## How many of us finish?
What percentage of purchased/downloaded online courses have you completed? Non-fiction paper books? Digital books? Bookmarks made while in scroll mode, for later "study mode" time?

Studies say 10-15%. (For me, it's worse, partly due to Udemy deals!).

Lack of time, lack of motivation, and lack of interest?

I think it is deeper than that. Writing this article is an attempt to evolve my perspective on the issue and solutions in this area.

Sources: 
* Speaking with a few others 
* Researching the topic online
* My own experience

## Days in the lives of many among us ("I")
When I say "I" here, I speak for many.
### A day that lights a fire under my feet
This is a rare day. Something inspires me to start a journey. Perhaps I see a new article or press release that expresses a vision that I perceive as credible.

I believe it is worth investing time and money in. Sometimes, there is a product right there in the article (along with a discount) that promises to help me position myself favorably in future. Sometimes, I have sought out the product on a different website, as step 1 of working on the vision.

The acquisition feels like a "no-brainer". And I buy/borrow/subscribe without using much of my "brain" ! Apple pay is so frictionless. All it takes is a jitter in my thumb to press the button twice and a zombie stare at my phone to make faceID work. And the product is sold. (Wait. Was I the product here ?)

I may install an app and login as well. I might even do a welcome session and lesson one.

Internally, this feels like I've accomplished something. After all, every 1000 mile journey starts with the first step, my mind says !


### A day of reckoning
This is also a rare day. Something reminds me that I'm behind on one of the growth areas I thought I'd focus on. 

It maybe an email reminding me of a discounted subscription period ending or a reminder email from the library or a reminder that the next class in a series is tomorrow 

I realize that time has passed and I haven't put in the effort I thought I'd put in, when I signed up for this.

I have to reckon with the fact that I've broken a promise I made to myself (and perhaps spent money.). 

Quickly, the mind says it isn't worth spending time in sorrow. The mind brings up a few sayings/proverbs quickly and diverts attention away from even considering a path towards sorrow, but instead into damage control measures.

I ask for a renewal/grace of expiry period if that's possible. The mind treats this as an action/achievement. 

If no extension is possible, I resolve to just do better next time.  

If there is an actual upcoming event (a class, a presentation, a test, etc) and the deadline is not movable, do the best I can in a short time frame to prepare for the event. I've been able to do well in many activities with just the last minute effort. Usually (not always), only I know that its not my best work, especially if the group doesn't know me well. 

The mind acts quickly during the day of reckoning, to ensure that the actual reckoning never happens ! Emotional expression which is perhaps needed for reckoning, is blocked by action.

### A usual day
For every vision, between the rare days that light my spirit on fire and days that splash cold water on my lazy face, there are the usual days.

Every day has some time that's not at my discretion. 

* Work that I've committed to at my professional place of work, kids pickup, dog care, cooking if I've committed to cook today, house/yard work or any other work that my spouse (or HOA !) has established a deadline on.
* There is some time that members of a family spend together talking, eating, playing, etc which is theoretically discretionary but for this article, I will consider it not so.
* If I've developed habits of exercise, meditation, hobbies (in areas unrelated to growth focus areas), these are great, but not considered discretionary time for this article.
* If I have a daily commute, I might have scammed myself into believing that no growth content is viable during commuting.
* There maybe a social event or ticketed event that my spouse and I have committed to attending.

And then, there are the few hours that are actually at my discretion. What happens to this time ?
Mostly this:
As part of being human, I have an innate need to connect to the world. 
- Some of this desire came from school where we were praised for good "general knowledge". 
- Some from socially being rewarded for being interesting, by being able to participate in varied topics of conversation.
- There has been actual life transforming outcomes from having skills that are way more than what was taught in college.

There are so many businesses that are trying to exploit this innate human need. Solo podcasters to billion dollar media companies, with their own niche topics designed to catch my attention with their storytelling.

I scroll youtube and quickly find a video that catches my attention. And then the next... Podcasters and the youtube app have got very good at eye-catching images, great storytelling and frankly, very good research and content too. 
But they spend 15 minutes of my time telling me something that I could read in 1 minute.

I may read things on twitter. If I read just the feed under my "following" tab, I might feel a FOMO and decide to just spend a few minutes on the "for you" tab.

I may read comments and the teacher in me may decide to spend time educating people (but they maybe bots !)

By the time I've seen what is happening around the world, my mind may feel like it accomplished something. If the topics were heavy, it may even convince me to watch some plain entertainment drama for a while.

Hours have passed. I've traveled around the world. But I've stayed still on growth focus areas. And in the age of planned obsolescence, standing still is walking backwards.

I then start on one of my areas of growth. Perhaps I spend a few minutes on it. After 5 minutes, it may actually catch and hold my attention and I would have stayed for a couple of hours, if I could.

However, it is already time for one of the things on my non-discretionary list. I have to stop the growth related activity and go do the "gotta do" thing.  And getting back to the growth activity only happens after I've paid another round of donation of my attention to the billion dollar attention seeking industry.

## What I should learn from this
It appears that I'm susceptible to time hijack when listening to podcasts or entertainment online. It can happen when reading things too.

It appears that I'm good at paying attention to things but the world is hijacking this attention. I'm literally "paying" attention, with my time, which is my life (potential).

I have to be careful on what I pay attention to and very carefully timebox anything that is not growth related, if I have committed to growing.


## What I should do (new behaviors)

I remember the days when there was no internet / no feed content on the internet. I'd have seek information in the form of book rental or magazine subscriptions. In both cases, I'd physically pick up something and it would finish within the time I'd estimate it would.

And I was clear on what I wanted to do and I did it (mostly).

I built stuff.

Now: 
- I have to refrain from video format news. Perhaps I should use the "AI summary" feature on youtube that can give me a text summary of a 15 minute video that I can read in 15 seconds !
- I have to schedule time and do things at that time.
- I have to give up on "general knowledge" and "being able to participate in conversations on various topics". I have to choose a few areas and stick to those.
- If I cant give up on "general knowledge", I have to time box it and should read AI summary only. The timebox should be small enough to not feel like an achievement to have read the stuff.

## Previous attempts at fixing myself have failed
I have known that I should pay attention in areas that I would like to grow in during my discretionary time. That is common sense.

However, I've not really analyzed what I do. I've fallen victim to the attention grabbing industry, without even realizing that I'm using up my discretionary time !

## How about an accountability group
Having an accountability group can help in crucial ways:
- I will be forced to say whether I was able to pay attention time to things I said were important to my growth.
- If I got hijacked on a day, I'll have to admit it at the very next accountability sync, which should be perhaps within a day or two?
- Knowing that I'll be held accountable will make me aware in real time, and I'll rarely waste my attention on things that dont matter.
